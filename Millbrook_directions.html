Driving

From Exeter M5 junction30, follow signposts to Exmouth, along the A376.
Turn right at the traffic lights in Lympstone next to the Saddlers.
Turn left after ~150m into Longbrook Lane, following the blue sign saying "Unsuitable for large vehicles".  (This is known locally as Curly Lane!)  After ~200m there is a sharp bend left.
Turn left 20m after the bend into Millbrook Cottage drive and left again into Millbrook Cottage.

By 57 bus

The 57 runs frequently Exeter – Topsham – Lympstone (Saddlers) – Exmouth – Brixington.

Walk from the Saddlers, following the driving directions above.

By train

During the day & evening there are ~half-hourly trains from Exeter St Davids, via Exeter Central, to Lympstone & Exmouth.
We are 15min from Lympstone Village station: Here are two of the many walking routes – 1 can be muddy after heavy rain, 2 is drier if you stick to the road:  

1.      Walk under the bridge (through the old farming end of Lympstone), past the Redwing & church, and up a slight hill.
Turn right at X-roads into narrow lane. Cross stream on footbridge by ford. Turn left round shed.
Follow footpath to road (at sharp bend mentioned above); turn right & Millbrook is on left.

2.      Walk through the old fishing end of Lympstone, past the Swan. (Watch out for bikes!)

a)       Except at high tide, go down the narrow Strand onto the beach, and walk round to Sowden End;

b)       If the tide is in, walk along the road.
  Enjoy views of Exmouth, Powderham etc.  

From Sowden End follow the lane inland; after a couple of bends and junctions it becomes Longbrook Lane.  Millbrook Cottage is the 2nd entrance on the right.

By bike

The Exe trail goes through Lympstone.  From Exeter you arrive at the station – follow 2b. above.
